How to Understand The 4 Bridge Rectifer.

The 4 bridge rectifier or full bridge rectifier is a circuit that turns AC (Alternating Current) into DC (Direct Current). (Later I will explain why turning AC to DC is important). To understand the four bridge rectifier you need to learn how Diodes work. Diodes are electrical components that act like a open switch when current passes through them one way, or act like a closed switch when the current passes through them one way. The Diode has two pins. One pin is called the Cathode and one pin is called the Anode. The Diode acts like a conductor or a closed switch when electrons flow from the Anode to the Cathode, this lets the current pass through the Diode. But when the Diode is reversed and the electrons flow from the Cathode to the Anode, the Diode acts like a open switch or a insulator, this stops the current from passing through the diode.
